Anthropic Mythos & AI Security

Signal vs. Noise

An internal assessment for ISD/EAG Security — April 2026

Gauthier Lawny, Senior Cybersecurity Consultant

ISD/EAG Security, Microsoft

What Is Claude Mythos?

Claude Mythos Preview is an unreleased general-purpose AI model built by Anthropic. It is not a cybersecurity product — its security capabilities emerged from general improvements in code understanding, reasoning, and autonomous multi-step task execution.

Anthopic describes it as capable of 'surpassing all but the most skilled humans at finding and exploiting software vulnerabilities.'

Key facts:

  • Not publicly available — restricted to vetted partners and security researchers
  • Not trained specifically for cybersecurity — capabilities are a side effect of scale
  • Announced April 7, 2026 alongside Project Glasswing
  • Name comes from Ancient Greek mythos ('narrative') — the stories civilizations used to make sense of the world

Anthopic chose not to release it broadly, citing the risk of misuse. Whether that caution is genuine, strategic, or both is a central question of this briefing.

What Is Project Glasswing?

Project Glasswing is Anthropic's initiative to channel Mythos's capabilities toward defense rather than offense. Announced simultaneously with the model, it provides gated access to Mythos for security work.

The deal:

  • $100M in Mythos API usage credits (not cash grants — this drives API adoption)
  • $4M in direct donations to open-source security (OpenSSF, Apache Foundation)
  • 12 founding partners: AWS, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorganChase, Linux Foundation, Microsoft, NVIDIA, Palo Alto Networks
  • 40+ additional organizations that maintain critical software infrastructure
  • 90-day public report commitment on vulnerabilities found and lessons learned

How you get access:

  • Glasswing partners get Mythos Preview directly
  • Everyone else: Opus 4.7 (public, with cyber safeguards) + a Cyber Verification Program for legitimate security researchers who need the guardrails lowered
  • Pricing: $25/$125 per million input/output tokens

Microsoft's role: MSRC evaluated Mythos on the CTI-REALM benchmark, reporting 'substantial improvements.' Available as a gated research preview on Microsoft Foundry.

What Anthropic Claims Mythos Can Do

These claims come from Anthropic's own Frontier Red Team. They are detailed and technically specific — but they are self-reported by the model's creator.

Flagship vulnerabilities discovered:

Target What Mythos Found Notable Detail
OpenBSD (27 yrs old) Remote crash via TCP signed-integer overflow Cost: <$50 per scan run
FFmpeg (16 yrs old) Out-of-bounds write in H.264 decoder Fuzzers hit the same line 5M times without catching it
FreeBSD (17 yrs old) Unauthenticated root RCE via NFS 20-gadget ROP chain across 6 RPC requests
Linux kernel Nearly a dozen privilege-escalation chains Chains 2-4 vulnerabilities, <$1-2K each
Browser engine Full sandbox escape 4-vulnerability chain with JIT heap spray

The headline number: On Firefox, Mythos produced 181 working exploits vs. 2 from the previous model — a claimed 90× improvement. The jump isn't in finding bugs (the previous model already found 22 CVEs in two weeks). The jump is in weaponizing them into working exploits.

N-day automation: Given a CVE ID and a git commit, Mythos can write a working exploit fully autonomously — no human in the loop.

What's Been Independently Verified

The UK AI Safety Institute (AISI) — a government body that tests frontier AI models before release — ran its own evaluations. AISI is not an Anthropic partner; it has a mandate from the UK government to assess AI risks independently.

  • 73% success on expert-level CTF challenges that no model could solve before April 2025
  • First model to complete a 32-step simulated corporate network attack end-to-end (3 out of 10 attempts)
  • Performance was still improving at their maximum 100M-token budget — it hadn't plateaued
  • Critical caveat from AISI themselves: 'We cannot say whether Mythos would succeed against well-defended systems' — their tests had no active defenders and no alert penalties

What independent practitioners are seeing — people with no financial stake in Anthropic:

  • Greg Kroah-Hartman (maintainer of the Linux kernel stable branch, one of the most senior open-source developers in the world): 'Something happened a month ago and the world switched. Now we have real reports. They are good and they are real.'
  • Daniel Stenberg (creator and sole maintainer of curl, used by virtually every internet-connected device): 'Less slop but lots of reports. Many of them really good. I am spending hours per day on this now.'
  • Simon Willison (respected independent tech blogger and Django co-creator): Independently verified the OpenBSD SACK bug via git blame — the vulnerable code was 27 years old. His take: 'Saying our model is too dangerous to release is a great way to build buzz, but in this case I expect their caution is warranted.'

The trajectory is the most credible data point — confirmed by AISI (independent) and the UK National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC, government):

Period What Happened
Mid-2024 Best AI completed <2 steps of a 32-step attack
Feb 2026 Opus 4.6 averaged 15.6 steps — 6× improvement in 18 months
Apr 2026 Mythos averaged 22 steps, completing the full chain 3/10 times

NCSC conclusion: 'These limitations are not permanent barriers.'

What's Marketing

Bruce Schneier — Harvard Kennedy School fellow, author of Applied Cryptography and Schneier on Security, widely regarded as one of the most influential voices in information security for three decades — calls it 'mostly marketing hype':

'They're convincing a lot of people that Mythos is this amazing step change in capability when the evidence right now… is that it might not be.'

His argument: every vendor overstates the threats their product solves. 'Too dangerous to release' creates mystique and drives demand. The real test is whether anyone else can replicate the results independently.

AISLE — an AI security startup and direct Anthropic competitor (so they have an incentive to show alternatives work) — ran Mythos's flagship findings against 8 different models:

  • 8 out of 8 models detected the FreeBSD RCE, including a tiny 3.6-billion-parameter model costing $0.11 per million tokens
  • Rankings reshuffled completely across different tasks — there is no stable 'best model for cybersecurity'
  • Their thesis: 'The strongest version of the narrative — that this depends on a restricted frontier model — looks overstated.' The moat is the system (scaffolding, orchestration, pipelines), not any single model

Caveat: AISLE's tests gave models the vulnerable function with hints — an upper bound, not an autonomous scan. They concede Mythos's multi-round exploit chaining is genuinely unique.

The 'too dangerous to release' playbook:

Lab Model Year What Happened Next
OpenAI GPT-2 2019 Released fully 8 months later
OpenAI GPT-4 2023 Red team report on 'power-seeking' → released with mitigations
Meta LLaMA 2023 'Gated release' → leaked within a week
Anthropic Mythos 2026 We are here

The pattern: Announce danger → demonstrate responsibility → release or get overtaken → capabilities democratize anyway.

Follow the money: Anthropic is pre-IPO. 'Too dangerous to release' is the ultimate credibility signal for investors. $100M in API credits (not cash) drives platform adoption. Every Glasswing partner's stock rallied after the announcement. The 90× number, the $100M figure, and the 12-partner coalition are all optimized for headlines.

The Verdict: What's Real, What's Noise

What survives scrutiny:

✅ The capability trajectory is real — 6× improvement in 18 months, confirmed by an independent government body (AISI)

✅ Exploit construction at scale is new — chaining 2-4 vulnerabilities autonomously, grinding through tedious exploit steps without fatigue

✅ The cost floor has collapsed — £65 per attack attempt (AISI), <$1,000 for a kernel privilege escalation

✅ Non-experts can leverage it — Anthropic staff with no security background got working remote code execution exploits overnight

✅ Real practitioners (Kroah-Hartman, Stenberg) independently confirm a step change in AI-generated vulnerability reports

What doesn't hold up:

❌ 'Uniquely dangerous' is overstated — 8 out of 8 models (including tiny ones) detected the flagship vulnerabilities

❌ The 90× Firefox number has zero independent replication — it's a self-benchmark by the model's creator

❌ 'Thousands of zero-days' — over 99% are undisclosed, making the claim unfalsifiable

❌ No testing against active defenders — AISI explicitly says they don't know if it works against a real SOC

❌ Every source has a commercial angle — Anthropic (IPO), partners (stock rally), even AISLE (competitor selling alternatives)

Bottom line: The capability is real. The trajectory is accelerating. But 'only Mythos can do this' is the marketing — this is the direction of the entire field, not one model. Prepare for the trajectory, not the brand name.

What This Means for Corporations

The patch window is closing

When an AI can take a CVE identifier and a git commit and produce a working exploit fully autonomously for under $2,000, every unpatched vulnerability becomes a race against automation. Palo Alto Networks' Unit 42 already observes the fastest AI-assisted attacks going from initial access to data exfiltration in 25 minutes — most enterprises take days to detect an intrusion.

90-day patch cycles are built for human-speed adversaries. That era is ending.

Mid-tier threat actors just got an upgrade

The skill gap is compressing. Anthropic reports that staff members with no security background received working remote code execution exploits overnight. If the capability trajectory holds, within 12-18 months mid-tier threat actors — financially motivated groups, hacktivists, disgruntled insiders — will have access to exploit capabilities that previously required nation-state resources.

As Palo Alto's CTO put it: 'A single bad actor can now run campaigns that once required entire teams. The models do not pause for sleep, they scale, and they only have to be right once.'

Defender advantage is real — but conditional

The UK National Cyber Security Centre (the UK's government authority for cybersecurity, equivalent to CISA) has a nuanced position: defenders can shape the battlefield because AI-generated attacks currently trigger detectable alerts. But this advantage is not guaranteed — it only works where monitoring exists and foundations are strong.

Their key insight: AI amplifies both strengths and weaknesses. Organizations with strong security baselines will benefit from AI-powered defense. Organizations with weak foundations will face amplified exposure.

The strategic question for every CISO: Are you in a position where AI helps you, or where AI helps your attacker more?

What This Means for Security Consultants

Vulnerability assessment is commoditizing

When a model can find a Firefox UAF in 20 minutes and a kernel privilege escalation in half a day for under $1,000, the value of manual vulnerability scanning and penetration testing shifts fundamentally. The volume of AI-generated findings will overwhelm traditional triage — Daniel Stenberg is already spending hours per day processing AI-submitted reports to curl.

What this means for our engagements:

  • Routine vuln scanning becomes a commodity — clients will expect AI-assisted coverage as the baseline
  • Manual pentest value moves up the stack: architecture review, threat modeling, detection engineering, incident response design
  • Our ability to interpret, prioritize, and contextualize AI findings becomes the differentiator, not the finding itself

Client advisory needs to evolve

Clients will ask about Mythos specifically. The right framing:

  1. Don't dismiss — the trajectory is independently validated and accelerating
  2. Don't panic — no model has been tested against active defenders; real-world impact in defended environments is unproven
  3. Don't fixate on the brand — this is a field-wide trend, not one model; prepare for the capability class, not the product name
  4. Focus on what they control — patching cadence, attack surface management, monitoring coverage, detection engineering

A note on our position

Microsoft is a Glasswing founding partner and an Anthropic investor. MSRC has evaluated Mythos and reports 'substantial improvements.' We have a seat at the table — and a commercial interest in the narrative being true.

Be transparent about this with clients. Our credibility as consultants depends on honest, source-aware assessment — not on amplifying a partner's marketing. When we recommend AI-assisted tools, recommend the capability class (frontier models for code review and vuln discovery), not a specific vendor.
